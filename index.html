01 / 03
the reframe
I showed ₹7,788/year.
Not ₹649/month.
Subscriptions are sold monthly because monthly feels affordable. The pain is annual — and it's designed to stay invisible. Showing the annual number isn't a UX choice. It's the product's entire argument: you're not spending ₹649. You're spending ₹7,788. Every year. Without deciding to.
What surprised me: users knew the monthly price of every subscription. Nobody knew the annual total. Not even close.
02 / 03
the trust problem
SMS permission is the most sensitive ask on a phone. I designed it to come last.
Asking for bank SMS access on screen one kills trust before it's earned. So I flipped the onboarding: enter your first subscription manually, feel the product working, then the prompt appears — "set this up to get the full picture." The ask lands after the value, not before it.
Still unsure: UPI autopay cancellation is fragmented across bank apps, NPCI, and the merchant. I've solved when to cancel. Not where.
03 / 03
the irony
A subscription app for people with too many subscriptions. Will they pay?
The answer depends on user archetype — not one price fits all. Casual users get 3 months free, then ₹49/month. Power users who want full control get a ₹199 one-time option. The product has to prove it saves more than it costs before it asks for anything.
SubSafe would show up in its own subscription list. I priced it so it's the first one worth keeping.
